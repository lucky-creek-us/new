Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about water pressure and our services

What causes low water pressure in my home? +

Low water pressure can have several causes, including:

  • Mineral buildup in pipes or fixtures
  • Partially closed main water valve
  • Problems with the pressure regulator
  • Corroded plumbing pipes
  • Municipal water supply issues
  • Simultaneous water use in multiple locations

Our comprehensive pressure analysis can identify the specific cause in your home.

How much does a water pressure analysis cost? +

Our standard water pressure analysis starts at $99. This includes:

  • Pressure testing at multiple locations
  • Flow rate measurement
  • Visual inspection of accessible pipes
  • Pressure regulator assessment
  • Detailed report with recommendations

If you proceed with any recommended services, the analysis fee is typically applied toward the total cost.

What is the ideal water pressure for a home? +

The ideal residential water pressure is between 45-65 psi (pounds per square inch).

Pressures below 40 psi may result in weak flow, while pressures above 80 psi can damage pipes, fixtures, and appliances. Many plumbing codes require pressure reducing valves when municipal pressure exceeds 80 psi.

We can measure your home's pressure and make adjustments if needed.

The duration of a pipe replacement project depends on several factors:

  • Single fixture repair: 2-4 hours
  • Section repiping: 1-2 days
  • Whole-house repiping: 3-7 days

We provide a detailed timeline during our initial assessment and work to minimize disruption to your household.
